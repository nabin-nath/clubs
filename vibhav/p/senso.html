Description:

Objective:

To detect illegal logging of trees and prevent deforestation. It can also be used in Forest Surveillance.

Abstract:

This project aims to tackle illegal deforestation by correlating sound from forests to an already cached dataset sound of logging machineries. Based on Digital Signal Processing, the foremost concept of this project is to detect the frequency of chainsaw/equipment employed for cutting trees, used for illegal deforestation.

Execution:

• Senso accumulates sound signals from surroundings and check whether it matches with the already available dataset sound of logging machineries.
• If the sound signal matches statistically to a numerical threshold, concerned authorities are instantly notified.
• Detailed reports incorporating graphs of time domain and frequency domain as evidence of logging are also send.
• Consequently, illegal deforestation is ceased in immediate effect.

Applications:

• Many communities rely on forest resources for a range of nutritional and medicinal purposes.
• Deforestation destroys essential ecosystem services like the provision of clean water and fertile soils, leading to the loss of farming and other livelihood opportunities, such as fishing and hunting for food.
• With the aim of terminating illegal deforestation, Senso intents to create a positive social impact on miscellaneous sections of society affected by it.
